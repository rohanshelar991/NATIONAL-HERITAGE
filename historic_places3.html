Historic Places of Rajasthan

Explore Rajasthan's Rich Heritage

Rajasthan is renowned for its rich history, and the state is dotted with numerous historic places that offer a glimpse into its royal past.

Famous Historic Places

City Palace

Overlooking Lake Pichola, the City Palace in Udaipur is one of the largest royal complexes in Rajasthan. Built by Maharana Udai Singh II in the 16th century, it is a blend of Rajasthani, Mughal, and European architectural styles.

Hawa-mahal

Hawa Mahal, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, is one of Jaipur's most iconic landmarks. The palace was designed for royal women to observe street festivals while remaining unseen. Its unique five-story fa√ßade with 953 small windows (jharokhas) is a fine example of Rajput architecture.

Chittorgarh Fort

Chittorgarh Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most historic forts in India. It is famous for its association with legendary figures like Maharana Pratap, Rani Padmini, and Meera Bai. The fort symbolizes Rajput valor, sacrifice, and honor.

Nahargarh Fort

Perched on the Aravalli hills, Nahargarh Fort was originally built by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1734 as a retreat. The fort offers stunning views of Jaipur city, especially at sunset. It is connected to Jaigarh Fort and Amber Fort through its fortifications.
