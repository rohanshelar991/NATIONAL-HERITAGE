Festivals of Assam

Celebrate Assam’s Vibrant Festivals

Assam is known for its vibrant festivals that reflect the cultural richness and diversity of the region. From traditional celebrations to grand events, Assam’s festivals are a colorful expression of its heritage and traditions.

Popular Festivals

Bihu

Bihu is the most celebrated festival in Assam, marking the Assamese New Year and the harvest season. The festival is celebrated with traditional dances, songs, and feasts, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Assam.

Raas

The Raas Festival, also known as the Raas Leela, is a traditional dance festival celebrated during the full moon night in November. It features performances of traditional dance forms and devotional songs dedicated to Lord Krishna.

Dol

The Dol Festival, also known as the Dol Jatra, is celebrated with great enthusiasm in Assam. It marks the arrival of spring and involves colorful processions, traditional dances, and the throwing of colors.

Tea Festival

The Tea Festival of Assam is celebrated to promote the tea industry of the region. The festival includes tea tasting events, cultural performances, and exhibitions showcasing the heritage and significance of Assam’s tea culture.
