Historic Places of Kerala

Explore Kerala's Rich Heritage

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," boasts a rich cultural heritage that is reflected in its historic places. From ancient temples to colonial forts, these sites tell the stories of Kerala's past. Notable historic places include the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, famous for its architectural grandeur, the Bekal Fort, offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea, and the historic Mattancherry Palace, which showcases the region's unique cultural fusion. Each of these sites provides a window into Kerala's vibrant history and diverse traditions.

Famous Historic Places

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Located in Thiruvananthapuram, the Padmanabhaswamy Temple is one of the richest temples in the world. It is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is renowned for its intricate Dravidian architecture. The temple is an important pilgrimage site and a significant part of Kerala's history.

Bekal Fort

Bekal Fort, built in the 17th century, located in Kasaragod is one of the largest and best preserved forts in Kerala. This historic monument offers a superb view of the Arabian Sea from its tall observation towers, which had gigantic cannons till a few centuries ago.
