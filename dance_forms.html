Traditional Dance Forms of Assam

Explore Assam's Dance Forms

Assam is known for its vibrant and diverse dance forms that reflect the rich cultural heritage of the state. Each dance form is performed during festivals and special occasions, showcasing the unique traditions and customs of Assam. Here, we present some of the most celebrated dance forms of Assam, each with its own distinct style and history.

Traditional Dance Forms

Bihu Dance

Bihu Dance is performed during the Bihu festival to celebrate the Assamese New Year. It involves energetic movements and traditional music.

Sattriya Dance

Sattriya Dance is a classical dance form that originated in the Vaishnavite monasteries of Assam. It is characterized by its graceful movements and religious themes.

Manipuri Dance

Manipuri Dance is a folk dance from the Goalpara region of Assam, known for its rhythmic steps and vibrant costumes.

Bituli Dance

Bituli Dance is a traditional dance performed by the Bodos and other communities of Assam, reflecting their cultural heritage and social values.
