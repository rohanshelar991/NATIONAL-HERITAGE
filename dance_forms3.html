Traditional Dance Forms of Rajasthan

Explore Rajasthan's Dance Forms

Rajasthani dances are a vibrant and integral part of the state's rich cultural heritage.

Traditional Dance Forms

Ghoomar Dance

Ghoomar is perhaps the most famous dance of Rajasthan, often performed by women on auspicious occasions like weddings and festivals. It involves graceful, swirling movements, with women wearing colorful, flowing ghagras (skirts) and covering their faces with veils.

kalbeliya Dance

Kalbeliya dance is associated with the Kalbeliya community, known for their expertise in handling snakes. The dance movements are fluid and resemble the graceful swaying of a snake.

Bhavai Dance

Bhavai is a skilled dance form that involves balancing multiple pots or other objects on the head while performing. It is mainly performed by women from the Bhil community.

Terah Taali Dance

Terah Taali is a devotional dance performed by the Kamad community, dedicated to the deity Baba Ramdev. The dancers tie thirteen cymbals (Manjeeras) to different parts of their bodies and strike them in rhythm.
