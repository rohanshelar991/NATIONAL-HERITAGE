Historic Places of Maharashtra

Explore Maharashtra's Rich Heritage

Maharashtra is home to a rich heritage of historic places, including the magnificent Ajanta and Ellora Caves, UNESCO World Heritage sites known for their ancient rock-cut temples and intricate carvings. The imposing Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus in Mumbai is a fine example of Victorian Gothic architecture. The Gateway of India stands as a colonial-era monument, while Shaniwar Wada in Pune reflects the grandeur of the Maratha Empire. Raigad Fort and Sinhagad Fort are iconic fortresses associated with the legacy of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. The state's heritage is a blend of ancient architecture, Maratha valor, and colonial influences, making it a treasure trove for history enthusiasts.

Famous Historic Places

Raigad Fort

Raigad Fort, once the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, is a majestic hill fortress known for its strategic location and historical significance.

Gate way of India

The Gateway of India is an iconic Mumbai landmark, built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911, and serves as a symbol of the city's rich history and architectural heritage.

Ambabai Temple

The Ambabai Temple in Kolhapur, also known as the Mahalakshmi Temple, is a revered shrine dedicated to the goddess Mahalakshmi, renowned for its ancient history, architectural beauty, and spiritual significance.

Ajanta and Ellora Caves

The Ajanta and Ellora Caves are ancient rock-cut temples in Maharashtra, famed for their exquisite sculptures, intricate paintings, and historical significance as a major center of Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain art and architecture.
