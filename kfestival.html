Festivals of Kerala

Celebrate Kerala’s Colorful Festivals

Kerala, often referred to as "God's Own Country," is famous for its vibrant and diverse festivals. These festivals reflect the rich cultural heritage and traditions of the state, celebrated with grandeur and community spirit.

Popular Festivals

Onam

Onam is the most important festival in Kerala, celebrated to honor the homecoming of the legendary King Mahabali. The festival is marked by traditional feasts, boat races, dance performances, and the creation of elaborate floral designs called Pookalam.

Vishu

Vishu marks the beginning of the Malayalam New Year. The festival is celebrated with special rituals, including the Vishukkani, and the distribution of Vishukkaineetam, which involves giving money to family members.

Thrissur Pooram

Thrissur Pooram is a grand temple festival held in the city of Thrissur. The festival is known for its magnificent elephant processions, vibrant fireworks, and traditional percussion music performances.

Attukal Pongala

Attukal Pongala is a famous women’s festival in Kerala, where millions of women gather to prepare a special offering for the goddess Attukal Amma. The festival is noted for its large-scale participation and the spirit of devotion.
