Traditional Dance Forms of Jammu and Kashmir

Explore Jammu and Kashmir's Dance Forms

Jammu and Kashmir, known for its breathtaking landscapes, is also rich in cultural heritage, including a variety of traditional dance forms. These dance forms are an integral part of the region's festivals, weddings, and other celebrations, showcasing its unique cultural identity. Here, we present some of the most celebrated dance forms of Jammu and Kashmir, each with its own distinct style and significance.

Traditional Dance Forms

Rouf Dance

Rouf is a traditional dance performed by women during festivals like Eid and other joyous occasions, known for its elegant footwork and graceful movements.

Bhand Pather

Bhand Pather is a traditional folk theatre of Kashmir that combines dance, drama, and satire to depict social and political themes.

Dumhal Dance

Dumhal is a vibrant dance performed by men of the Wattal tribe, characterized by colorful costumes and the use of large ceremonial banners.

Hafiza Dance

Hafiza is a classical dance form traditionally performed at Kashmiri weddings, accompanied by the melodious strains of traditional music.
