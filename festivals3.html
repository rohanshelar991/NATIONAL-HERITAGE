Festivals of Rajasthan

Celebrate Rajasthan's Vibrant Festivals

Rajasthan, India's largest state, is known as the "Land of Kings" and is famous for its royal heritage, vibrant culture, and stunning landscapes. From the golden deserts of Jaisalmer to the majestic forts and palaces of Jaipur, Rajasthan offers a glimpse into India's rich history. The state is renowned for its colorful festivals, traditional attire, folk dances like Ghoomar, and delectable cuisine, making it a captivating destination for cultural exploration and historical discovery.

Popular Festivals

Pushkar Mela

The Pushkar Camel Fair is one of the largest camel fairs in the world.The fair is also a significant pilgrimage for Hindus, who come to bathe in the holy Pushkar Lake.

Desert-Festival

The Desert Festival in Jaisalmer showcases the rich and colorful culture of the Thar Desert. The festival includes folk dances, traditional music, camel races, turban-tying competitions, and a Mr. Desert contest.

Gangaur Festival

Gangaur is one of the most significant festivals in Rajasthan, dedicated to Goddess Gauri, a manifestation of Parvati.

Mewar Festival

The Mewar Festival, celebrated in Udaipur, is a major event marking the arrival of spring and coincides with the Gangaur festival. It reflects the cultural heritage of the Mewar region.
