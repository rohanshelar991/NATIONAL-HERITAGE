Jammu and Kashmir is renowned for its beautiful landscapes and rich cultural heritage, which is vividly expressed through its vibrant festivals. These celebrations, deeply rooted in tradition, reflect the unity and diversity of the region.

Popular Festivals

Lohri

Lohri, celebrated primarily in Jammu, marks the end of winter and the onset of the harvest season. The festival is characterized by bonfires, traditional songs, and dances, bringing communities together in celebration.

Baisakhi

Baisakhi is a significant festival in Jammu, celebrating the harvest season and the Sikh New Year. It is observed with processions, religious prayers, and cultural performances.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Eid-ul-Fitr is a major festival in Kashmir, marking the end of Ramadan. It is a time for prayers, feasts, and communal harmony, with families coming together to celebrate.

Shivratri

Shivratri, also known as Herath in Kashmir, is an important Hindu festival. It is celebrated with religious fervor, involving prayers, fasting, and traditional rituals dedicated to Lord Shiva.
