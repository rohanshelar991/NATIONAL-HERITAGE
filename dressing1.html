Traditional Dresses of Maharashtra

Discover the Traditional Attire of Maharashtra

Maharashtrian dressing reflects the state's cultural heritage, with men traditionally wearing the Dhoti or Dhotar paired with a Kurta or Pheta (turban). Women typically wear the Nauvari Saree, a nine-yard saree draped in a unique style that allows freedom of movement. The attire is often complemented by traditional jewelry, including the Nath (nose ring), Mundavalya (forehead ornament), and green glass bangles, especially worn during weddings and festivals. This traditional dressing symbolizes the rich cultural identity of Maharashtra.

Famous Traditional Dresses

Nauvari Saree

The Nauvari Saree is a traditional nine-yard saree worn by Maharashtrian women, draped in a unique style that allows ease of movement and exudes grace and elegance.

Dhoti Kurta

The Dhotar, Kurta, and Pheta are traditional Maharashtrian attire for men, with the Dhotar being a wrap-around garment, the Kurta a long shirt, and the Pheta a turban that symbolizes honor and pride.

Parkar Polka

The Parkar Polka is a traditional Maharashtrian outfit for young girls, consisting of a long skirt (parkar) and a matching blouse (polka), often adorned with vibrant colors and patterns.
